The event bears a remarkable resemblance to The Trolley Problem, a well known meta-ethical thought experiment in which an oncoming trolley imperils the lives of five rail workers operating in a narrow tunnel. The agent in the thought experiment sees the situation, as they stand behind a sixth man, who is innocent, but fat and oblivious to their presence. The agent and the fat man stand atop a bridge with no guard rails that passes over the tracks. The agent must then decide whether to save the lives of the five rail workers by pushing the fat man off the bridge and onto the tracks to stop the trolley.

When asked about his working relationship with The Trolley Problem, Mortimer Black barked defensively

Obviously it was a train rather than a streetcar so the Trolley Problem model can’t be applied here. No consequentialist scholar would mistake this situation for a trolley problem, obviously.
